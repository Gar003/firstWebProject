Sardar Bhagat Singh

1907-1931

Warrior

Bhagat Singh was an Indian revolutionary who played a pivotal role in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule. He is widely regarded as a martyr and a symbol of the Indian freedom movement. Early Life and Activism Born in 1907 in Punjab, Bhagat Singh was deeply influenced by the nationalist movement from a young age. He was drawn to revolutionary ideas and joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA), an organization dedicated to overthrowing British rule through armed struggle.   Key Actions and Beliefs Bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly: In 1929, Bhagat Singh and his comrades threw bombs inside the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi to protest against the oppressive British policies. This act, though symbolic, garnered significant attention and sparked a nationwide debate.    

Biographies

  • Hunger Strike: While imprisoned, Bhagat Singh embarked on a hunger strike to protest against discriminatory treatment of Indian political prisoners. This act further solidified his status as a symbol of resistance.
  • Socialist Ideology: Bhagat Singh was influenced by socialist ideas and advocated for a revolutionary transformation of Indian society.He believed in the power of the masses and the need for social justice
  • Martyrdom and LegacyBhagat Singh, along with his fellow revolutionaries Sukhdev Thapar and Shivaram Rajguru, was executed by the British colonial government in 1931 at the age of 23. Their martyrdom ignited a wave of patriotism and inspired countless Indians to join the struggle for independence.
